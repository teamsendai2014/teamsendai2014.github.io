Materials and Methods

Simulation Condition

Enzymatic Device (Simulation)

Values of chemical parameters are as follows.
Hybridization:kh=3.0×108[LM-1min-1]
Denaturation:kd=1.0×105[min-1]
Branch migration:kb=1.0×105[min-1]
Polymerase:kp=17[min-1]
Nickase:kn=3.0[min-1]
Restriction enzyme:kr=3.0[min-1]

These parameter are referenced from followings.
- Zhan and winfree (2009)
-K.Montagne, R.Plasson, Y.sakai, T Fujii, Y. Rondelez, Mol.Sys.Biol.7 (2011) 1-7
Our model neglects reverse reactions for the sake of simplicity.

Enzyme Free Device (Simulation)

Values of chemical parameters are as follows.
Seesawing reactions :ks=5.0×104 [M-1s-1]
Toehold binding reactions : kf=2.0×106 [M-1s-1]
Toehold binding reactions :krf=10 [s-1]

These parameter are referenced from followings.
-Lulu Qian, Erik Winfree: Supporting Online Material for “Scaling Up Digital Circuit Computation with DNA Strand Displacement Cascades”, Science, 332, 1196--1201, 2011

Functionality

2-instruction operation

  1. Input-AB or BA/Template complex, Transducer, and Gate were formed by annealing (cooling from 95 to 20℃ for 75min, -1℃/min). All samples were mixed after the formation of complexes.
  2. Enzymatic reaction was conducted for various time (0, 1/6, 1/2, 1, 2, 5, 10, 12, 15, 20, 25, 30, 40 min), then these were left at 80℃ for 30 minutes. After that, all the samples were annealed again for 35 minutes (cooling from 95 to 70℃ for 25min, -1℃/min, then cooling from 70 to 20℃ for 10min, -5℃/min).
  3. 15% acrylamide gel was formed with 1×TBE buffer.
  4. The electrophoresis was conducted at 100V for 85 min.
  5. Imaging by gel imager with Green light.

Reaction speed measurement

 Inspired by a paper[3], we apply FRET to detect the release of Output-A. Before the releasing, the 6-FAM does not glow because the fluorescence of 6-FAM is absorbed by BHQ-1. In the presence of Signal, Output-A is released, and then 6-FAM glows. Therefore, we can confirm whether Output is released or not.

Fig.5 Detection of Output-A by using FRET

  1. Input/Template complex and Output complex were formed by annealing (cooling from 95 to 20℃ for 75min, -1℃/min). All samples were mixed after the formation of complexes.
  2. Enzymatic reaction was conducted for various time (1,2,5,10,20,50 min), then were kept at 80℃ for 20 min. After that, all the samples were left at room temperature until the time of applying (more than 1 hour).
  3. 15% acrylamide gel was formed with 1×TBE buffer.
  4. The electrophoresis was conducted at 100V for 75 min.
