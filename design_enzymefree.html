Design

Enzyme free device

In this page, we explain the device using only DNA.

In this page, we explain the mechanisms of enzyme free device. Enzyme free device is designed based on seesaw gates capable of cascading strand displacement reactions.

Components of the device are Input , Trigger, Gate, Reporter and Fuel.
The order to release outputs is only coded in the input, while other DNA components is utilized for cascading the reactions.



Cascading reactions are implemented by following steps.

Step1. Trigger hybridizes with Input. A signal is then released by strand displacement reaction.

Step2. The signal released in Step1 hybridizes with Reporter. A first output is released by strand displacement.
This reaction takes place faster than other reactions so that Reporter serves as a function of threshold.

Step3. After Step2 finished, remaining Signal starts to hybridize with Gate. As a result, a new trigger is then released. Signal is used as a catalyst in this step by the function of Fuel which hybridizes with Signal/Gate complex and restores Signal. By consuming the excess Fuell , the reaction progresses from a small amount of Signal.

Step4. The new trigger hybridizes with input. A new Signal is then released which starts next cascades.
