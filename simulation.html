Simulation

Enzymatic device

Many components in the Enzymatic device may result in combinatorially large number of intermediate structures. To predict the behavior of the device, we formalized ordinary differential equations (ODE) model based on chemical kinetics of the processes. The numerical simulation was carried by mathematical software (Scilab), while the ODEs were derived by writing down all the possible intermediate reaction by hand (more than 400 equations). Details are available at Source_code, All_intermidiates, Chemical_parameters.



Fig.1 Simulation results of 2-instruction operation (left:A then B right: B then A). Initial concentrations of all the components are set at 200nM. The horizontal and the vertical axes are time and concentration, respectively.

We have conducted two simulations with Input A then B (Fig.1 left) and B then A (Fig.1 right). These results indicate that the order of Output is programmable.




Fig.2 Simulation result of 3-instruction operation (A then B then C). Initial concentrations of all the components are set at 200nM. The horizontal and the vertical axes are time and concentration respectively.

Next, we increased the number of Instruction from two to three. The result is shown in Fig.2. Three Outputs were properly released in order (A then B then C). Even the model contains more than 400 intermediates, our elaborate design makes the device possible to behave as programmed.



Fig.3 Relationship between Input concentration and time interval. Time intervals were evaluated for various Input concentrations (10nM ,20nM, 50nM, and 100nM). The horizontal and the vertical axes are time interval and concentration, respectively.

Moreover, time intervals between releases can be controlled by tuning the concentration of Input. We define the output-time as the time when 50% of the Output is released. We measured the time between consecutive output-time. Fig.3 shows the relationship between Input concentration and the time intervals.



Fig.4 Relationship between Template-B concentration and time interval. (Template-B concentration was 10nM, 20nM, 50nM and 100nM). The horizontal and the vertical axes are time interval and concentration, respectively.


Time intervals (between 1st and 2nd, between 2nd and 3rd) can be independently controlled by changing concentration of Templates or Gates. For example, Fig.4 shows relationship between Template-B concentration and time interval. More data about controlling time intervals are available here (Template-A Template-C All Gats Gate-A Gate-B ) .

From the simulation results, we confirmed the following functionalities and features of Enzymatic device.

  • programmability of the permutation of Outputs
  • scalability of the number of Outputs
  • controllability of the time intervals between Outputs releases

Enzyme-free device

As well as Enzymatic device, we formalized ordinary differential equations based on chemical kinetics of Enzyme-free device. The numerical simulation was carried out by mathematical software (Scilab). ODEs were derived by writing down all the possible intermediates by hands. Source codes, workflows of chemical reactions and more information are available here ( source_code_1, source_code_2, All_intermediates, Chemical_parameters). By simulation, we have analyzed the performance of the Enzyme-free device.
