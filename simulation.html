Simulation of Enzyme device

Simulation (enzyme)
In this page, we explain about simulations of enzyme device.
Enzyme device includes some DNA which have partly complementary sequences one another. Thus there is a possibility that undesirable intermediates are produced. However it is difficult to predict the behavior of them in experimental analysis. Here, we made a mathematical model by formalizing the chemical reactions for the simulation.
In this project, the model releases at most three different DNA in order. A Source code of the model, workflow of all chemical reactions and other infomati are followings. (source code, workflow other information )
By analyzing this model, we checked the followings.
„Éªeffects of undesirable intermediates
„Éªprogrammability of the permutation of Outputs
„Éªprogrammability of the number of Outputs
„Éª programmability of the time intervals between Outputs


First, we simulated the model which releases two outputs. Two patterns of simulation are implemented in this case.
One is Input instructs the device to release first Output-A then Output-B. The other is Output-B then Output-A.
The results is shown in Fig.1.


Fig.1 results of simulation of model which releases two ouputs (left:A then B right: B then A). Outputs are released in order. Permutations of outputs are changed. All concentrations of components are 200nM.

In these simulations, the device released Output as Input coded. The device demonstrated the programmability of the permutation of outputs.


Second, we simulated the model which releases three outputs. Input instructs the device to release three outputs.
The result is shown in fig.2.


Fig.2 result of simulation of model which releases three outputs. All concentrations of components are 200nM.


Three outputs are released as we designed. This result means that the number of output is programmable.
In both simulations, the device performs without problems. In theory, our device satisfies followings.
?effects of undesirable intermediates
?programmability of the permutation of Outputs
?programmability of the number of Outputs


Moreover, we analyzed the model of another conditions because the time intervals of each output depend on the concentrations of each component.
Tuning the Input concentration is one way to program the time intervals. Fig.3 shows that various time intervals corresponding to Input concentration. Increasing Input concentration results in shortening a length of the time interval.
Another ways of programming the time intervals are tuning the concentration of Templates or Gates. When all Templates are tuned equally, the time interval is changed in the same way as Input.(Fig.4)
Of course, the time interval between arbitrary Outputs can be also programmed by changing the concentration of relevant components. For example, the time interval between Output-A and Output-B is changed depending on the concentration of Template-B. (Fig.5)
Another results of the simulation are here.(the time intervals corres to Template-A Template-C All Gats Gate-A Gate-B )


Fig.3 changes of the time interval corresponding to the concentration of Input. Time intervals were measured in various Input concentrations.(10nM ,20nM, 50nM and 100nM). The time interval is changed in the range of 10sec from 5sec in this model.
(Rhombus: time interval between Output-A and Outut-B Square: time interval between output-B and Output-C triangle: time interval between output-A and Output-C)


Fig.4 changes of time intervals corresponding to Templates concentration. time intervals were measured in various Templates concentration (all Templates were 10nM, 20nM, 50nM and 100nM). The time interval was changed in range of 15sec from 5sec.


Fig.5 changes of the time interval between Output-A and Output-B. Only time interval between Output-A and Output-B can be changed corresponding to Template-B. (Template-B concentration was 10nM, 20nM, 50nM and 100nM)


From these results, we checked the following.
?programmability of the time intervals between Outputs

Eventually, the model demonstrated followings in simulations.
„Éªthe device releases outputs in order.
„Éªthe permutation, number and time interval of outputs are programmable

We achieved following two sub-goals.
?Formalize chemical reaction for the models
?Confirm the behavior of the model

„Éªprogrammability of the time intervals between Outputs

Enzyme free device satisfies the followings as well as Enzyme device.
„Éªthe device releases outputs in order.
„Éªthe permutation, number and time interval of outputs are programmable

Second, we simulated the model which releases three output to demonstrate programmability of the number of Outputs(Fig.2). the device performed as we designed.

Fig. 2 simulation of model which releases three Output. The device releases three Outputs in order. Input and components are arranged so that the device can repeat cycles three times.

From the above simulations, the followings are checked.
?programmability of the permutation of Outputs
?programmability of the number of Outputs

Next, we analyzed the time intervals of each Output by using the model which releases three Output.
The time intervals between arbitrary Outputs are programmed by changing relevant components as well as Enzyme device.


Fig.3 show the time intervals corresponding to various Input concentrations. The concentration of Input has influence on each time interval. In Enzyme free device, The time intervals of each Output are changed equally by adjusting all Gates concentration at the same time. (Fig.4)

Fig.4 time intervals corresponding to Gates concentrations. The time intervals between each Outputs are equally changed in the range of about 10 sec from 3sec.


From these results, we checked the following.
?programmability of the time intervals between Outputs
