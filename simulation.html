Simulation

Enzyme device

Since the components of the Enzyme system can result in combinatorially large number of intermediate structures, it is difficult to predict the behavior of the device. In addition, it is laborious to reveal the relationship between concentration of components and time intervals of output DNA by repeating chemical experiments. To overcome these problems, we formalized ordinary differential equations (ODE) based on chemical kinetics model to simulate the behavior of the device in a reproducible manner. The numerical simulation was carried by mathematical software (Scilab), while the ODEs were derived by writing down all the possible intermediates by hand (more than 400). Raw materials, all intermidiates and more information are available [here]. (Source_code, All_intermidiates Chemical_parameters )


We ran two types of simulation. One is a simulation of A then B while the other is B then A. The results are shown in Fig.1.

Fig.1 Results of simulation of model which releases two outputs (left:A then B right: B then A).All concentrations of components are 200nM. The horizontal and the vertical axes are time and concentration respectively.

These results indicate that the order of Output is programmable because the order to release the Outputs can be coded only in the Input.
Second, we increased the number of output from two to three. The result is shown in Fig.2.

Fig.2 Result of simulation of model which releases three outputs. All concentrations of components are 200nM. The horizontal and the vertical axes are time and concentration respectively.

Three Outputs were released in order (A then B then C). This result means that we can program the number of Output. Even the model contains more than 400 intermediatesour, our elaborate design makes the device possible to behave as we design.

Moreover,the time intervals between Outputs can be controlled by tuning the concentration of Input or Templates or Gates. We define the output-time as a time when 50% of the Output is released. Then, time interval is defined as a difference between two output-times. Tuning concentration of Input is a simple way to control the time interval. Fig.3 shows the relationship between Input concentration and the time interval.

Fig.3 Relationship between Input concentration and the time interval. Time intervals were measured in various Input concentrations.(10nM ,20nM, 50nM and 100nM). The horizontal and the vertical axes are concentration and time intervals respectively. Rhombus, square, and triangle indicate output-time of Output-A, time interval between Output-A and Output-B, time interval between Output-B and Output-C respectively.


In this case, time intervals (between first and second, between second and third) are both controlled together (homogeneous control). Both time interval range from 5sec to more than 10sec. When we change the concentration of Templates or Gates, the time intervals can be controlled separately. (heterogeneous control).For example, Fig.4 shows the relationship between Template-B concentration and time interval.

Fig.4 Relationship between Template-B concentration and time interval. (Template-B concentration was 10nM, 20nM, 50nM and 100nM). The horizontal and the vertical axes are concentration and time interval respectively. Rhombus, square, and triangle indicate output-time of Output-A, time interval between Output-A and Output-B, time interval between Output-B and Output-C respectively.


Only time interval between Output-A and Output-B can be changed corresponding to Template-B in a range of about 10sec from 6sec.
More date about time interval are available here (Template-A Template-C All Gats Gate-A Gate-B ) .
By analyzing this model, we comfirmed the following features.

  • programmability of the permutation of Outputs
  • programmability of the number of Outputs
  • programmability of the time intervals between Outputs

Enzyme free device

As well as Enzyme device, we formalized ordinary differential equations based on chemical kinetic model to simulate the behavior of Enzyme free device. The numerical simulation wes carried by mathematical software (Scilab). Of cource, ODEs were derived by writing down all the possible intermediates by hands.

Source codes, workflows of chemical reactions and more informion are available here( source_code_1, source_code_2, more_information ). By simulating the models, we analyzed the performance of the Enzyme free device.

At first, we simulated the model which releases two outputs. The results are shown in Fig.5.


.
Fig.5 Simulations of model which releases two Output. The concentration of Input is 200nM. All Templates concentration is 200nM. All Transducer concentration is 100nM. All Gates concentration is 200nM. All Fuels concentration is 400nM. These simulation were achieved by using source_code_1

Output were released in order as we designed. This results means we can program the order of Outputs as well as Enzyme free device.
Second, we simulated the model which releases three output(Fig.6).

Fig.6 Simulation of model which releases three Output. The concentration of Input is 200nM. All Templates concentration is 200nM. All Transducers concentration is 100nM. All Gates concentration is 200nM. All Fuels concentration is 400nM. This simulation was achieved by using source_code_2.

The device releases three Outputs in order (A then B then C). This result indicates that the number of Outputs is programmable.


Next, we analyzed the time intervals of each Output. The time intervals between arbitrary Outputs are programmed by changing relevant components as well as Enzyme device. Fig.7 shows the time intervals determined by various Input concentrations. The time interval between Output-A and Output-B is changed in the time width from 4sec to 8sec, while the time interval between Output-B and Output-C is changed in the time width from 12sec to 18sec.
The time interval between Output-B and Output-C is changed by adjusting Gate-B concentration. (Fig.8)
More data about the time intervals are available here. (Changing Gate-A,All_Gates,all Fuels,Fuel-A,Fuel-B,Trigger )

Fig.7 The time intervals corresponding to Input concentrations. Rhombus, square, and triangle indicate output-time of Output-A, time interval between Output-A and Output-B, time interval between Output-B and Output-C respectively.

Fig.8 Time intervals corresponding to Gate-B concentrations. Rhombus, square, and triangle indicate output-time of Output-A, time interval between Output-A and Output-B, time interval between Output-B and Output-C respectively.

By analyzing models, we comfirmed the following features.

  • programmability of the permutation of Outputs

  • programmability of the time intervals between Outputs
