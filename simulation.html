About Enzyme device

Since the components of the Enzyme system can result in combinatorially large number of intermediate structures, it is difficult to predict the behavior of the device. In addition, it is laborious to reveal the relationship between concentration of components and time intervals of output DNA by repeating chemical experiments. To overcome such problems, we formalized ordinary differential equations (ODE) based on chemical kinetics model to simulate the behavior of the device in a reproducible manner. The numerical simulation was carried by mathematical software (Scilab), while the ODEs were derived by wring down all the possible intermediates by hand (more than 400!!). Raw materials and all intermidates date are available [here]. (Raw_material, All_intemidiates other_information )

First, two types of simulation were implemented. One is that the device releases Output-A and Output-B in order, while the other is Output-B and Output-A in order. The results are shown in Fig.1. Fig.1 results of simulation of model which releases two ouputs (left:A then B right: B then A).All concentrations of components are 200nM.

These results indicate that the order of Output is programmable because the order to release the Outputs can be coded only in the Input.
Second, we increased the number of outputÔºéThe result is shown in Fig.2. Fig.2 result of simulation of model which releases three outputs. All concentrations of components are 200nM. All concentrations of components are 200nM.

Three Outputs were released in order(A then B then C). This result means that we can program the number of Output.
Even the model contains more than 400 intermediatesour, our elaborate design makes the device possible to behave as we design.

Moreover,the time intervals between Outputs can be controlled by tuning the concentration of Input or Templates or Gates.We define the output-time as a time when 50% of the Output is released. Then,time interval is defined as a difference between two output-times.
Tuning concentration of Input is simple way to controll the time interval. The time interval depending on Input concentration is shown in Fig.3.


Fig.3 changes of the time interval depending on the concentration of Input. Time intervals were measured in various Input concentrations.(10nM ,20nM, 50nM and 100nM).

In this case, time intervals (between first and second, between second and third) are both controlled together (homogeneous control).
When we change the concentration of Template or Gate, the time intervals can be controlled separately. (heterogeneous control).For example, the time interval between Output-A and Output-B is changed depending on the concentration of Template-B.(Fig.4)

Fig.4 changes of the time interval between Output-A and Output-B. (Template-B concentration was 10nM, 20nM, 50nM and 100nM)

Only time interval between Output-A and Output-B can be changed corresponding to Template-B. Another date about time interval are available here. (Template-A Template-C All Gats Gate-A Gate-B )
