Discussion

Enzyme device

In this page, we discuss the difference between the results of simulation and experiment. As shown in previous page, the reaction velocity in simulation is 100 times bigger than in experiments. Disagreement between reaction kinetics in the experiment and rate constants in the simulation seems to result in the difference. In particular, we referred to [ ] articles [1,2] for the rate constants in the simulation. We assumed that the rate constant of hybridization between Signal and Gate was ten times smaller than that between Signal and Transducer. For an accurate analysis, fitting each rate constant to an experimental value one by one is required.
In the simulation, Signal DNAs are amplified without limit because we assume constant concentration of dNTP. Our model of the simulation can be refined if we take into account the saturation of Signal DNAs by formalizing Michaelis-Menten kinetics [3]. Moreover, our model ignores minor reactions such as denaturation of stable DNA duplex. Although the reaction kinetics of the denaturation should be negligibly small in real experiment, we still have the space to improve our model.
As described in the result of experiment,“ABC” or “CAB” can be released as output in order but the same output such as “ABB” or “ABA” cannot be released again in this Enzymatic device. The structure of this device has ample room for further improvement. We tried to fit data obtained from simulations and experiments, though fitting is a simple way. Considering the previously described problems, we changed the reaction rate constant of hybridization between Gate and Signal from 5.0*10^5 to 5.0*10^2, and that of hybridization between other substances from 5.0*10^6 to 5.0*10^4. We added decrease terms to ordinary differential equations so that they converge on a constant value.


Fig.1 Fitting result of simulation

The graph shown below is obtained by fitting. Comparing with the data obtained from experiments, we can guess that fitting was carried on sufficiently in a simple way.

Enzyme free device

The graph of simulation was sigmoid curve while that of experiment was convex upward. We guess that insufficient thresholding caused the results.


Fig.2 Result of simulation without the function of threshold
The thresholding is caused by the difference of reaction rate constant of two hybridizations: Signal to Output and Signal to Gate.
We set same number for that constant in this simulation.

The shape of graph is convex upward, which is similar to experimental plot. To get a sigmoid results in experiment, we may need to optimize the length of toehold so that we can overcome the problem of thresholding. Purification of DNA structures is another possible solution to deal with the problem because it can prevent stoichiometric inaccuracy. Such solutions may improve the performance of our Enzyme free device in the future.
