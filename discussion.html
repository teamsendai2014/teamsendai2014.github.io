Enzyme device

In this section, we contrast the result of simulation and experiments and study differences between these two results. As shown in in previous section, the reaction velocity in simulation is more 100 times greater than in experiments. Perhaps the cause of this difference is in reaction rate constant or reaction process in simulation. Rate coefficient of reaction in simulation is quoted from references. However, we equally decided the reaction velocity in all reaction except gate. The reaction velocity of hybridization with gate DNA is 10 times greater than that of the value. If we determine these reaction rate constant by using experiments and apply the values to simulation, we will achieve accurate simulation. In each process, some single strand DNAs are released. In simulation, they are released endlessly. However a quantity of dNTP is limited, so they converge on a constant value. Therefore if we add to reaction formulas of simulation to make Key DNA be a constant value, we can get accurate value. Moreover in simulation DNA denature reaction does not occur, but actually there are not a few denature reaction. We should experiment about this reaction and consider the impact in simulation.
